---
title: "On Taking Things Seriously"
date: 2026-03-16
tags: ["life", "reflections"]
---

It's fun to think about ideas. To imagine the things we could do, the places we could go, the things we could build. There is a magic in that - and it's often the first step towards something important. You can stumble around, maybe even take steps in that direction. The future is full of possibilities.

But there is a shift when you start taking that idea *seriously*.

In a somewhat trivial example, let's say you're thinking about going somewhere with friends. It's fun to research places, look up things you can eat.

But then that shift happens - are we really going to do this? That's when the constraints come in. How much is this going to cost? Do we have the time? What activities are actually worth it?

It also happens in business. Whether it's starting a business or taking on a new project, it's fun to ideate. Then things get *serious* - what can we *really* accomplish? By *when*?

The shift is perhaps most fraught in romantic relationships. At first, it's fun. But then, the constraints: where are we going to live? How will we spend our weekends? What should we spend money on? Do we want kids?

All of these things involve *tradeoffs* and *compromises*. They require you to ask: is this really *worth* it? They require difficult conversations. To put in work. To make plans.

This isn't always difficult - sometimes the decisions are easy and you effortlessly move forward.

But sometimes it is difficult. There is that feeling of *resistance*. Of *uncertainty*.

It's easy to just give up at that point - to think "*nevermind*." And sometimes that's the right decision. Sometimes the tradeoffs aren't worth it - or it just isn't feasible.

But sometimes it's the wrong decision. Sometimes that resistance is worth pushing through - and it *is* worth taking seriously.

It's important to not get overwhelmed at that moment. Take your time. Think it through. That isn't to say *go for it*. It's about being aware that you've shifted from the fun part to the more serious part - and that it feels *different*. And that's okay.

Step back, take a breath. Think. Then pick a path.
